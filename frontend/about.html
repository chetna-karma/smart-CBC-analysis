Understand the science, reference boundaries, and physiological significance of your blood values

Reference Ranges Reference Guide

The Complete Blood Count (CBC) is one of the most common blood tests. It measures the cells that circulate in your blood, providing general clues about your immune response, oxygen delivery, and coagulation profile.

Parameter Biological Role Standard Normal Range
Hemoglobin (Hb) Oxygen-carrying protein inside red blood cells. 12.0 - 16.0 g/dL
White Blood Cells (WBC) Immune defense cells fighting infections and pathogens. 4000 - 11000 /µL
Platelet Count (PLT) Cell fragments responsible for blood clotting and healing. 150000 - 450000 /µL
Red Blood Cells (RBC) Transport cells carrying oxygen to tissues and organs. 4.5 - 5.9 M/µL
Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV) The average size of a single red blood cell. 80.0 - 100.0 fL

General Health Tips

✦ Hydration

Dehydration can cause artificial elevation of red blood cell counts and hemoglobin concentrations. Stay well-hydrated before testing.

✦ Iron Absorption

Boost iron absorption by pairing iron-rich foods (beans, meats, spinach) with Vitamin C (oranges, bell peppers). Avoid coffee or tea with meals.

✦ Vitamin B12 & Folate

Required for proper RBC synthesis and division. Folate is found in dark leafy greens, while B12 is found in animal proteins and fortified cereals.
